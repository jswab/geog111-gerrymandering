Welcome to Redistricting Lab
Learn how political boundaries shape representation
1
Select a district to draw
Click a District 1–5 button in the sidebar. You must have a district selected before painting — if you click with Erase active on an empty cell, you'll get a reminder.
2
Paint cells onto the map
Click a cell to assign it. Click-drag to paint many cells. Click an assigned cell again to deselect it. Districts must stay contiguous — no islands. Blocked moves flash red.
3
Right-click for a quick assign menu
Right-click any cell to open a menu and assign it to any district without switching tools.
🌊
The River — cross only at bridges
Each map has a river (dark blue cells) running through it. Districts cannot cross the river except at the two bridge cells (gold/tan). Hover a bridge cell to confirm. In the ⚡ Gerrymandering? scenario, this rule is lifted so you can experiment freely — you'll see a notice when you switch to it.
4
Read the race dots
Each cell shows colored dots: ● White  ● Black  ● Hispanic  ● Asian. The number is voter population.
5
Aim for equal populations
Each district should have roughly the same voters — the "one person, one vote" principle. Within 5% of the ideal triggers a celebration popup, and the next district is auto-selected.
6
Analyze your map
Click Analyze ▶ to see a full fairness verdict: population balance, compactness score (Polsby-Popper), and racial representation — including whether minorities were packed or cracked.
💡 Try ⚡ Gerrymandering? to draw intentionally unfair maps with no river restrictions — then Analyze to see the consequences.
